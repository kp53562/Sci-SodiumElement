Sodium - The 11th Element in the Periodic Table of Elements.

The map below shows where sodium is found in the world.

Image credit: Britannica

Sodium is found nearly everywhere in the world. Salt is an extremely common compound that sodium is found in.
In fact, pure metallic sodium is much less common and is much more expensive than salt due to its reactivity (see properties).


Salt and other sodium compounds are everywhere in the world. The average concentration of salt in oceans on Earth is 3.5%,
which means there are over a hundred billion kilograms of salt in just a cubic mile of water (which is around one trillion gallons).


Some countries which produce metallic sodium or salt(sodium chloride) in large amounts:

  • China
  • France
  • Spain
  • Italy
  • Japan
  • India
  • The United States

    • Many other countries produce salt, but it is very hard to list them all because there are around 195 countries in the world.
