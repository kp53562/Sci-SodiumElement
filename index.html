Sodium - The 11th Element in the Periodic Table of Elements.

The alkali metal sodium is the second highest on the Periodic Table of Elements, in the alkali metals family. The English chemist Humphry Davy discovered sodium in 1807 after electrolyzing caustic soda, better known as lye. Sodium has a single valence electron like other alkali metals, meaning that it is extremely reactive.





Historical Facts!

Sodium’s name comes from the Medieval Latin word for “headache remedy”, or “sodanum”.
Humphry Davy, the chemist who discovered sodium, also went on to discover many other elements such as calcium, potassium, and boron.
