Sodium - The 11th Element in the Periodic Table of Elements.

Sodium is a very soft, reactive metal. It appears silvery-gray and shiny when unoxidized but tarnishes rapidly in air. It conducts electricity and heat well,
and is elastic, though still very soft and malleable.



Sodium is so soft, it can be cut with a knife.




As you can see, cutting the sodium exposes the inside where it has not yet oxidized.
It can be easily cut with a knife, but reacts very quickly with air, so the inside will soon oxidize.
