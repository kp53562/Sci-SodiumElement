Sodium - The 11th Element in the Periodic Table of Elements.

There are numerous useful compounds that sodium can form, including sodium hydroxide, or lye, and sodium chloride, or table salt.
Because of its reactivity, especially with air and water, it is rarely used in its pure form, but some nuclear reactors use liquid sodium as a coolant (It has impressive thermal conductivity, sodium's properties are further explained in the properties page); for the same reason, it is also used in science demonstrations.


Lye (Sodium Hydroxide, NaOH)

  • Sodium was first extracted and discovered through the electrolysis of this element.
  • Lye was used even as long ago as 2800 B.C. in making soaps because of its ability to saponificate fats and oils.
  • Lye can be created through the infamous reaction between sodium and water, also releasing combustible hydrogen gas in the single replacement reaction.
    Because of this and how difficult it is to get pure metallic sodium due to its reactivity, lye is usually produced through electrolysis of salt and water instead.

Baking Soda (Sodium Bicarbonate, NaHCO₃)
